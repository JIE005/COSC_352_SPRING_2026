# Project 08 – Data Correlation Analysis

## Dataset 1
**Name:** Part 1 Crime Data  
**Source:** https://data.baltimorecity.gov  
**Description:**  
This dataset contains records of major crimes in Baltimore, including details such as crime type, time, and location.  

**Key Columns Used:**  
- Neighborhood  
- CrimeDateTime  
- Description  

---

## Dataset 2
**Name:** Vacant Building Data  
**Source:** https://data.baltimorecity.gov  
**Description:**  
This dataset includes information about vacant and rehabilitated buildings in Baltimore, including their location and status.  

**Key Columns Used:**  
- Neighborhood / Community  
- Property status  

---

## Research Question
Do neighborhoods with higher numbers of vacant buildings also experience higher crime rates?

---

## Answer
The analysis shows a general relationship between vacant buildings and crime levels. For example:

- OLIVER → Crimes: 673 | Vacant Buildings: 324  
- CENTRAL PARK HEIGHTS → Crimes: 805 | Vacant Buildings: 256  
- BETTER WAVERLY → Crimes: 602 | Vacant Buildings: 113  

These results suggest that neighborhoods with a higher number of vacant buildings tend to also have higher crime counts. While not every neighborhood follows this pattern, there is a noticeable trend indicating a positive correlation between vacancy and crime.

---

## Limitations
- The datasets may not cover the exact same time periods  
- The data does not prove causation, only correlation  
- Some records were skipped due to formatting issues  
- Neighborhood names may not perfectly match across datasets  

---

## Conclusion
There appears to be a correlation between vacant buildings and crime rates in Baltimore neighborhoods. Areas with more vacant buildings often show higher crime counts, suggesting that vacancy may be associated with increased crime activity.
